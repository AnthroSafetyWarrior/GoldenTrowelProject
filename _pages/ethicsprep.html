Title IX/Ethics and Institutional Support


If something happens during fieldwork, you have the right to institutional support.


This page links to Title IX offices (U.S.) and comparable ethics or safeguarding offices worldwide. These teams can help you understand your options, connect you with confidential advocates, and guide you through accommodations or reporting. Offices are listed by country and institution for easy access.

Register Your Travel

Share your itinerary with your institution and (optionally) your government‚Äôs travel registry so they can reach you during emergencies, natural disasters, or evacuations.

  • Institutional travel registry: File your trip with your university‚Äôs travel/risk office (dates, sites, supervisors, local contacts).
  • Emergency contacts: Add on-site leads, local partners, embassy/consulate numbers, and your home Title IX/ethics office.
  • Medical & meds: List allergies, key conditions, and where you‚Äôll access care and refills abroad.
  • Itinerary updates: If plans change (location, housing, field site), send a quick update to the same contacts.
  • Document copies: Keep digital and paper copies of passport/ID, visas, insurance, and IRB/permits.
  • Privacy note: Share only what‚Äôs needed; ask how your data is stored and who can view it.

How Title IX & Ethics Offices Can Help

Your institution‚Äôs Title IX or equivalent office focuses on safety and academic continuity. You can contact them whether an incident occurs on campus, off-site, or abroad.

Supportive Measures

  • No-contact directives and safety planning.
  • Housing, lab, or field-site adjustments.
  • Referrals to confidential advocates and counselling.
  • Help connecting to local medical/legal resources (on-site or abroad).

Academic Accommodations

  • Extensions, make-ups, alternate assessments.
  • Remote options or adjusted timelines for fieldwork.
  • Coordination with faculty and supervisors on your behalf.
  • Leave/withdrawal options that protect standing and funding.

Process & Options

  • Explain reporting vs. non-reporting and confidentiality.
  • Outline informal and formal resolution paths.
  • Assist with documentation you may need (incident log, emails, travel notes).
  • Coordinate with international risk/travel offices for incidents abroad.

Scope & Limits (Expectations)

  • They advocate within your home institution but generally cannot compel external/host institutions.
  • They cannot guarantee legal outcomes; they can help you access local resources.
  • Jurisdiction may vary abroad‚Äîask how they‚Äôll coordinate support across locations.
