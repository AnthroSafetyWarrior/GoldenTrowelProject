Embassy & Travel Support


If something happens in the field or while abroad, your embassy / consulate can help you find safe options and navigate local systems.

Register Your Travel

  • Enroll your trip with your government’s traveler registry so officials can reach you during emergencies and you can receive alerts.
  • Share your itinerary (lodging, local contacts, transit) with your supervisor and trusted peers.
  • Store backups of passport, visas, insurance, and key phone numbers (printed + offline digital).

How can your embassy or consulate help?

Embassies and consulates exist to assist their citizens when problems arise abroad. While they cannot intervene in local investigations or legal proceedings, they can offer critical support such as:

  • Helping you replace a lost or stolen passport
  • Connecting you with local medical care, legal aid, and translation services
  • Assisting in contacting family, friends, or your home institution in emergencies
  • Providing lists of local attorneys and hospitals
  • Offering information on local laws and customs that may affect your safety
  • Coordinating with local authorities if you are detained, hospitalized, or a victim of crime

If you are conducting fieldwork, it’s recommended to register your travel plans with your country’s travel registry program so your embassy can reach you in the event of a natural disaster, political unrest, or emergency evacuation.
