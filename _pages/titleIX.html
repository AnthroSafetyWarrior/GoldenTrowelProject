This hub provides links to Title IX offices (U.S.) and equivalent ethics or reporting offices (international) for institutions with anthropology or related fieldwork programs. Protections apply to both students and faculty. Because terminology differs worldwide, we’ve grouped institutions by country and listed available reporting or ethics channels.

No institution affiliations: If you are not a member of the listed institutions, you may still report incidents that occur on campus or during official activities. For fieldwork abroad, also consult local laws and your home institution's reporting office.

[stuff]

[stuff]

Disclaimer: This is not an exhaustive or authoritative legal resource. Laws are subject to change. While the website publishers endeavor to update these resources when applicable, you should still contact university legal services or a qualified legal advisor to confirm the most current regulations before conducting fieldwork.

Reporting Ethics Violations to your Institution

This list currently includes reporting offices at universities and institutions with anthropology programs. If you need information for a specific institution, please let us know through our Website and Resources Feedback Survey. Your input helps us prioritize which resources to add first. Our long-term goal is to expand this hub beyond anthropology, supporting researchers across all fields in eradicating misconduct in the field.
