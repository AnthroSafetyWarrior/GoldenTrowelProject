I'm Concerned I May be Harassed or Assaulted

[stuff]

  • Complete the Fieldwork Safety Plan prior to your reserach trip.
  • try to move 1 on 1 meetings to public places, invite others to join if possible
  • inform parties present that you are recording the meeting for your safety
  • begin recording immediately (review state/country laws first)
  • if you detect escalation beyond appropriate interactions inform the individual(s) present that you are recording and will turn over any data to local authorities
  • DO NOT make any statements suggesting the individual(s) reputation, employment, status etc. will be compromised by the release of the recorded sexual misconduct. This constitutes extortion/blackmail and is often a felony crime.
  • DO NOT attempt to coerce the individual(s) into acquiescing to demands following the concealment of the recorded sexual misconduct. This constitutes extortion/blackmail and is often a felony crime.
  • following the reporting procedures listed below
Reporting to local authorities, your institution, the embassy, and your funding agencies offers a robust support system capible of applying any necessary pressure to ensure your case is being taken seriously. They can also negotiate a safe space for you to continue your research, facilitate your extraction, negotiate with the instution/fieldsite to remove that individual from power and from published works related to the data at their institution.

I Need to File a Report

Whether you are conducting research domestically or internationally, there is a reporting procedure for your protection.

  • Immediately record the details of the interaction in as great of detail as you can even if you have a digital recording of the interaction.
    • If you have a digital recording of the incident, backup your data and share with your Fieldwork Safety Plan support person
  • Contact to your Fieldwork Safety Plan support person to put the Incident Response Plan in place
  • Contact the local authorities to report the incident.
