Recording Law Definition of Consent Summary
Safe and Inclusive Working Environments for Off-Campus or Off-Site Research Consent has been interpreted to mean “acquiescence or compliance [with the proposition of another].”

Lack of consent results from:
  1. forcible compulsion (includes accusers holding positions of authority); or
  2. being incapable of consent.
In addition, “Consent to engage in sexual intercourse, sodomy, sexual acts, or sexual contact may be communicated by words or actions. The existence of a current or previous marital, dating, social, or sexual relationship with the defendant is not sufficient to constitute consent. Evidence that the victim suggested, requested, or otherwise communicated to the defendant that the defendant use a condom or other birth control device or sexually transmitted disease protection, without additional evidence of consent, is not sufficient to constitute consent.”		 In Alabama, you can lawfully record private conversations or meetings you are party to for your protection.This applies to offensive and defensive recording.

Stating your lack of consent or otherwise making it clear you are being forced constitutes sexual violence.
